Bio

The first program I wrote was a BAC calculator on a TI-84 Plus in high school; I was a hit at parties… I took an elective to pursue my interest and made a Rubik’s Cube solver in Visual Basic. After years of programming as a hobby I decided to sharpen my skills through the Web Developer Track at Bloc, of which I am a recent graduate.

I recently took a Java program of mine for teaching users how to solve a Sudoku puzzle, translated it into JS, and turned it into the project below. I know I can figure out how to solve any stumbling block in completing projects and I have the innate desire to solve puzzles that propels me into doing just that. I am currently Cincinnati based, but very willing to travel or work remotely.

Portfolio

Sudoku Solutions

This application takes any given Sudoku and walks the user through the logic of solving it with detailed explanations at each step. It also has a feature to generate a random new puzzle of a specified difficulty. Accessible to A11y users, keyboard users, mouse users, and phone users. Also included is a brute force solve method.
